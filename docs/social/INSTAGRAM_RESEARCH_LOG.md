# Instagram Research Log

Use this file for raw research notes from other Instagram accounts. This is the working notebook beneath `INSTAGRAM_MEMORY.md`: capture what was observed, why it may matter, and whether it should change our strategy.

Keep this log factual and lightweight. Do not copy captions, scripts, layouts, or artwork. Extract reusable patterns only.

## How To Use

- Add one row per post, Reel, carousel, Story, or account-level observation.
- Prefer recent posts from the active target account set in `INSTAGRAM_MEMORY.md`.
- Record visible public metrics when available. If a metric is unavailable, write `not visible`.
- Use `Memory action` to decide whether the observation should change `INSTAGRAM_MEMORY.md`.
- Use `Draft action` to decide whether it should become a test in `INSTAGRAM_DRAFT_QUEUE.md`.
- After Well Within posts something inspired by a pattern, track the actual result in `INSTAGRAM_EXPERIMENT_LOG.md`.

## Field Guide

- `Date reviewed`: when we reviewed the reference.
- `Source account`: account handle.
- `Reference URL`: direct post URL when available.
- `Reference date`: original post date if visible.
- `Format`: Reel, carousel, static image, Story, profile/bio, caption pattern, or account-level observation.
- `Topic`: what the post is about.
- `Hook / first frame`: the opening idea or first-frame structure, summarized in our own words.
- `Visible metrics`: public views, likes, comments, shares, saves, or follower counts if visible.
- `Likely success driver`: why this may be working.
- `Audience language`: phrases, concerns, or desires the audience appears to respond to, rewritten in our words.
- `Well Within adaptation`: original idea we could test without copying.
- `Target metric`: reach, views, saves, shares, comments, profile visits, follows, link taps, or App Store clicks.
- `Claim / brand risk`: prediction, diagnosis, medical claims, fear, shame, proprietary method language, off-brand tone, copied creative, or none.
- `Pattern score`: high fit, medium fit, low fit, or reject.
- `Memory action`: none, reinforce existing bet, update memory, mark watch, mark stale, or reject.
- `Draft action`: none, add draft, update draft, retire draft, or test later.
- `Notes`: anything a future agent or Jim should know.
